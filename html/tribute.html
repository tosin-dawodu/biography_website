Taiwo Nuga|| Brother

My big brother, Broda mi, as I always called him left us so suddenly. I am still finding it difficult to believe that he is no more with us. Broda mi was a generous, kind, protective, loving and caring person he always wants to be sure that everyone was fine. When I was planning my trip to Paris, France for my studies, he offered to pay for my flight ticket. He said " Taiye I will be honoured if you allow me to buy your flight ticket " I can still relive our walk from Race Course to CMS where the flight agent was located. A few months later he came to see me in Paris, on his way to London to make sure that I was settling in well. Many years later, he came to London to see me and my young family. That's the nature of the man I knew. Very protective and caring for his siblings. I will miss the philosophical discussions, chats and jokes that we often have. Broda me enjoyed life to the full. He would often take me on a drive in his orange-brown beetle. For me, in my late teens at the time, life was 'cool' and he contributed a lot to that period of my life when I was getting to understand the 'adult' world. What a BIG brother!
I want to thank him for the positive impact he had on me. Being with him and knowing him, has made me a better person. He thought me how to be positive even when things are not going as planned. His usual phrase to me was always 'aburo mi, à dàà, Kàmàdùpè. Always remain hopeful and grateful no matter what. Lastly, I want to say that his love, care and pride in his children was beyond reproach. Thank you so much for being part of our lives. You will be sorely missed. Brôda mî, è sun ré o🙏🏾
