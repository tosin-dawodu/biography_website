Pa Ezekiel Olagbenga Dawodu

31/03/1947 to 14/01/2022

Ezekiel Olagbenga Ayodeji Dawodu was born on 31st March 1947 to the families of Late Pa Christopher Olatunde Dawodu , a foremost Timber Technologist of note from Ijebu Igbo, Ogun State and Sussanah Funsoh Campbell, the daughter of church Patriarch, Late J.G. Campbell, founder of the West African Episcopal Church with the headquarter at 76, Adeniji Adele Road, Lagos.

Education

For his elementary education, he attended Ade Oshodi Elementary School situated on Tapa Street, Lagos from 1952 - 1958 and on completion, proceeded to Muslim High School Sagamu, Ogun State but completed his secondary education at Epe Divisional Grammar School Epe, Ogun State where he sat for his WASCE and was successful.

Work Career

On completion of his secondary education, he was employed as a Clerk with the Federal Civil Service in 1967 in the Accounts department at the Ministry of Works headquarters on Race Course Lagos. His handwriting got a lot of approbation from all who worked closely with him. His mastery of the art of calligraphy drew admiration from his bosses and he was always asked to prepare the presentation of financial reports. This was the era when PowerPoint presentation was not in vogue. Added to this, he was a very conscientious and dedicated civil servant and this earned him respect from all; thus his career progressed. I can recall vividly that one of his bosses requested that he be transferred with him to AFDP - Armed Forces Development Project, an arm of the Federal Government that administered Armed Forces Projects. He served in the Federal Ministry of Agriculture, there, he was posted to the Federal Ministry of Internal Affairs, this particular ministry made him travel nationwide to different parts of Nigeria for auditing. His last assignment as a civil servant ended in the Federal Ministry of Education as the deputy chief auditing officer. Later on in life, he acquired the name Egerton and to his admirers he fondly address himself as Egerton Daw then EDA and later EDAC which he finally used as his business name EDAC Trades.

Training

At a stage in his career, he was sent on a long-term course to ASCON in Badagry, the then-popular Civil Service University of repute and on his return, he was promoted to higher ranks and on and on, and he became an Assistant Director. His last posting was to Abuja before he finally retired honourably on completion of the statutory years in service

Extra-Curricular Activities

He was a lover of sports with his best in football. He was a very good and agile goalkeeper while in secondary school and even after. He kept the goal for his school and was even invited by the then Western Region to represent the region in a competition. He held sway also for Okepopo Boys and Girls Club Lagos, a clubhouse we both belonged.

Retirement

He moved with his younger children to Odoragunshin, Epe in 2010 to retire from the hustle and bustle of the central city. He was instantly adopted by the community as one of theirs. ‘Baba Alaja’ as he was fondly called was a gregarious man and enjoyed the company of many within the community and beyond. He played host to his friends and neighbours, especially on Easter and Christmas celebrations. He was a cheerful, generous and compassionate giver of note and he gave to those in need as the Lord provided for him. He was also a devout member of the Catholic faith up to his passing and played vital roles in the development of the church where he was once the vice-chairman of the Church Council. He did not die unsung, he is survived by children, grandchildren and great-grandchildren who are making their mark in their different callings; brothers, sisters and the entire family. "O death where is thy sting? O grave where is thy victory. But thanks be to God which giveth us the victory through our Lord Jesus Christ". May his gentle soul rest in peace.
